Chapter 9

Sanctified to Step Into Calling

At Bethesda Tabernacle, sanctification was always tied to purpose. Members learned that being filled with Christ did not only restore identity—it empowered them to step fully into calling. Pastor Jason Barrett often preached: “Your story is being rewritten not just for your healing, but so that you can impact the world. Purpose flows from presence.”

One evening, the sanctuary was charged with expectation. Members arrived weary, burdened by past failures, fear of judgment, and uncertainty about their path. Pastor Jason Morgan called them into worship, declaring that God would reveal their identity and calling through the Spirit.

During worship, a young woman named Leah experienced a powerful vision. She saw herself standing before a vast field, each section representing opportunities, gifts, and acts of service. Shadows tried to block some areas, representing doubt, fear, and past failures. Suddenly, Jesus appeared, walking confidently across the field. Wherever He stepped, the shadows fled, and vibrant light filled the space. Leah felt herself being empowered to walk into every area Jesus had cleared.

Pastor Joey Martin observed multiple members being touched by the Spirit in tangible ways. Some fell to their knees, trembling as unseen hands guided them forward. Others felt warmth and weightlessness simultaneously, experiencing empowerment beyond physical understanding. Angels were visible to some, walking alongside members, affirming their gifts and callings.

Pastor Joseph Bennett taught the congregation about identity in Christ. He referenced Galatians 2:20: “I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me. And the life I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself for me.” He emphasized that stepping into calling requires a full awareness of God’s presence dwelling within. Identity and purpose are inseparable.

During extended prayer, Pastor Jason Barrett felt a prophetic vision for the congregation. He saw streams of golden light flowing from the sanctuary into the surrounding community. Each stream represented a member stepping into their unique calling—teachers, healers, ministers, innovators, and leaders. Angels moved along the streams, safeguarding their paths and confirming God’s favor.

A young man named Caleb, who had carried shame for years, saw himself standing on a mountain. At first, the path up the mountain seemed impossible—steep, jagged, and shadowed. Jesus appeared, extending His hand. “Step forward in Me,” He said. Caleb took one trembling step, then another. With each step, light filled the mountain, clearing obstacles. By the summit, Caleb felt a supernatural empowerment, an alignment of identity and purpose he had never known.

Pastor Joey Martin reminded the congregation that calling is not always public or immediate. Some callings unfold gradually. Some are expressed through service, prayer, and faithfulness in the small things. Every act of obedience positions a believer to receive greater revelation and empowerment.

During worship, several children experienced visions affirming their future purpose. One little girl, Miriam, saw herself leading a group of people through a valley of shadows. Angels flanked her on both sides, guiding her steps. Jesus walked ahead, showing her the path. She realized, even as a child, that her identity and purpose were sanctified and protected by God’s Spirit.

Pastor Joseph Bennett tied these experiences to Scripture, explaining that sanctification produces fruit. He referenced John 15:5: “I am the vine; you are the branches. Whoever abides in Me and I in him bears much fruit, for apart from Me you can do nothing.” Purpose and calling are birthed in the presence of Christ and confirmed by the Spirit.

Members were encouraged to identify areas of gifting and to step boldly into service. Pastor Jason Barrett reminded them: “Do not wait for perfection. Do not wait for approval. Your story has already been rewritten. Your calling is sanctified. Step into it with faith and boldness.”

By the end of the service, the congregation experienced a deep sense of empowerment. Members left with clarity about their gifts, courage to act, and assurance that God was actively guiding their steps. Bethesda Tabernacle had become a house where supernatural affirmation met practical purpose—where identities were restored, and callings were illuminated.

The chapter concludes with a shared vision among the pastors. They saw the sanctuary filled with radiant light, streams of golden energy extending into the community. Angels moved among the members, guiding them forward. Jesus stood in the center, smiling, saying: “I have written your story, I have restored your identity, and I have sanctified your purpose. Step forward in Me. Walk fully into your calling. The world waits.”
