Prologue

The story behind the vision that would become Bethesda Tabernacle.

The House They Were Looking For

Long before Bethesda Tabernacle had a name, a building, or a congregation, there was a question that refused to leave.

The question appeared in conversations that lingered long after they ended. It surfaced in quiet moments of prayer and in stories shared across coffee tables, hospital rooms, and church hallways. No matter how different the circumstances seemed on the surface, the same ache eventually revealed itself beneath the details.

How had so many people who loved Jesus come to feel so far from home?

Some carried wounds left by careless words spoken years earlier. Others had spent much of their lives trying to become the version of themselves they believed everyone expected. Many still prayed faithfully, opened their Bibles regularly, and held firmly to their faith in Jesus. Even so, a quiet distance remained between what they believed and what they experienced.

There were people sitting in sanctuaries every week who secretly wondered whether there was truly a place for them. There were people carrying questions they no longer felt safe asking and disappointments they no longer knew how to express. Beneath smiles, handshakes, and familiar routines, many were searching for something they could not quite name.

Those stories began settling deeply into the heart of Pastor Jason Barrett. One conversation became another. One testimony led to the next. Faces remained in his thoughts long after services ended, and names returned to him during prayer. The pattern became impossible to ignore.

People were not simply searching for answers. Many were searching for refuge. They longed for a place where healing mattered more than appearances, where honesty was welcomed rather than feared, and where the presence of Jesus remained at the center of everything.

As the burden grew, so did a conviction that the Church was meant to be more than a gathering place. Scripture described a people being transformed into the likeness of Christ, yet many believers had come to associate spiritual growth with fear, exhaustion, or endless striving. Sanctification had become a word discussed often, while the beauty of walking closely with Jesus was sometimes lost beneath expectations and performance.

Months of prayer followed. Questions led to seeking, and seeking led to deeper dependence upon God. During that season, Pastor Jason found himself returning repeatedly to the same prayer: Lord, show me what Your heart looks like for the people who feel forgotten.

The answer would arrive in a way he never expected.

A vision began to unfold.

That vision would become the foundation for everything that followed.

The pages ahead tell the story of that vision and the people whose lives were shaped by it. They tell the story of wounded hearts finding hope, weary souls discovering belonging, and ordinary people encountering the transforming presence of Jesus. Above all, they tell the story of sanctification as a living work of God, drawing people closer to Himself while restoring what fear, shame, and disappointment once tried to steal.

The journey begins before the church exists, before the first service is held, and before a single sermon is preached. It begins with a burden, a prayer, and a vision that would eventually become a house where countless people would discover that Jesus had never stopped walking toward them.
