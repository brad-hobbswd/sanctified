Chapter 1

The Vision at Bethesda

The sanctuary had grown quiet hours earlier, yet Pastor Jason Barrett remained near the front of the room. An open Bible rested on his lap, untouched for several minutes as his thoughts drifted toward the stories that had filled recent months. Faces returned to his memory with surprising clarity. Conversations that seemed ordinary when they occurred now carried a weight he could not easily dismiss.

The burden described in countless prayers had never centered on church attendance, ministry programs, or numerical growth. Something deeper had been stirring beneath those concerns. Again and again, he encountered people who loved Jesus sincerely yet struggled to find a place where they felt fully known. Their faith remained intact, but many carried wounds that had convinced them belonging was reserved for someone else.

Several days of prayer and fasting had brought him back to the sanctuary that evening. The building stood empty, free from the usual activity that filled every corner during the week. A stillness rested over the room that seemed almost sacred, as though the distractions of ordinary life had stepped aside long enough for him to listen.

As he prayed, the familiar surroundings gradually faded from his awareness.

A different scene began to emerge.

Stone walkways stretched before him beneath an open sky. The sound of moving water echoed through the air. People gathered around a pool worn smooth by generations of waiting, each carrying burdens that were visible in some lives and hidden in others. Some sat quietly against the surrounding walls. Others watched the water with unwavering attention, as though every hope they possessed depended upon what might happen next.

Jason immediately recognized the place.

He was looking at Bethesda.
